Streamer Diplomacy:
The Great China Journey

Understanding Global Reactions through Live Chat Data

Zerong Lu

01

The Phenomenon: Why This Matters

In late 2024, American mega-streamer IShowSpeed livestreamed his visit to China, exposing millions of global viewers to Chinese cities in real time.

Unlike edited media, live streams generate instant, unfiltered audience reactions through fast-moving chat messages.

Research Question:
When viewers watch China through a livestream, are chat reactions merely chaotic noise — or do they reveal meaningful patterns of global perception and curiosity?

02

The Toolkit: Taming the Noise

We transformed chaotic live chat into structured, interpretable data.

1. Scrape
1,000 live chat comments per city via the YouTube API
2. Clean
Removal of emojis, URLs, and high-frequency stop words using NLTK
3. Analyze
VADER sentiment analysis — a rule-based NLP model designed for social media — classifying comments as Positive, Neutral (descriptive), or Negative

03

Shanghai: The Vertical Metropolis

Audience Focus

Viewers repeatedly commented on urban scale, lighting, and futuristic aesthetics, often comparing Shanghai to other global Asian cities.

Interpretation

Frequent mentions of “Korea” suggest viewers frame Shanghai through familiar regional benchmarks, positioning it within a global hierarchy of modern cities.

04

Observational Neutrality (Shanghai)

55.9%

Neutral Sentiment

Neutral comments dominate because viewers primarily describe what they see rather than expressing strong judgments — acting as digital tourists.

05

Chongqing: The 8D City

Unlike Shanghai, the chat focuses less on comparison and more on Chongqing’s unique vertical geography and visual complexity.

Words such as “city” and “beautiful” dominate, indicating visual fascination rather than geopolitical framing.

06

Consistency Across Cities

60.1%

Neutral Sentiment

Despite visual differences, sentiment distributions remain strikingly similar — reinforcing the idea of livestream audiences as observers first, evaluators second.

08

Key Takeaways

Digital Tourist Gaze

Chats function as collective observation rather than political debate.

Curiosity Over Negativity

Positive sentiment consistently outweighs negative reactions.

Global Framing

Viewers interpret cities through global and regional reference points.

09

Limitations & Future Work

  • VADER struggles with sarcasm and streaming slang
  • No timestamp-level sentiment alignment
  • Future work: temporal and multilingual analysis
10

Conclusion

Livestreams are not just entertainment — they are windows into global perception.
